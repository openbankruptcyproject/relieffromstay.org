Motion for Relief from Stay

A plain-language guide to 11 U.S.C. Section 362(d) -- the process creditors use to ask the bankruptcy court to lift the automatic stay and resume collection, foreclosure, or repossession.

What Is Relief from Stay?

When a debtor files bankruptcy, the automatic stay under Section 362 halts nearly all creditor activity. But the stay is not absolute. Section 362(d) gives creditors the right to ask the court to lift the stay -- allowing them to proceed with foreclosure, repossession, lawsuits, or other collection actions despite the pending bankruptcy case.

There are four grounds for relief under Section 362(d). The most common are "for cause, including the lack of adequate protection" under 362(d)(1), and where the debtor has no equity in the property and the property is not necessary for an effective reorganization under 362(d)(2). The burden of proof is split -- the creditor must show cause, but the debtor bears the burden on equity and reorganization necessity.

Relief from stay motions are among the most frequently litigated matters in bankruptcy court. For debtors, losing a stay motion can mean losing a home, a vehicle, or critical business equipment. Understanding the grounds, the timeline, and the response options is essential.
